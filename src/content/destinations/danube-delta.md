Where the Danube finally meets the Black Sea, it fans out into Europe's
largest and best-preserved river delta — a UNESCO-listed maze of channels,
reedbeds, and lakes. You explore it slowly, by boat, gliding past pelicans and
herons toward villages with no roads in. There's not much to "do" here in the
usual sense, and that's exactly the point: it's one of the quietest, wildest
corners of the continent. Come to switch off, drift, and watch the birds.
